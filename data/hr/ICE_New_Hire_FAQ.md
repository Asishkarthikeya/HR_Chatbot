# ICE New Hire FAQ

This document answers the most common questions new employees ask during their first few weeks at Intercontinental Exchange (ICE).

## First Day & Getting Started

**Q: What time should I arrive on my first day?**
A: Arrive at **8:30 AM** at the ground floor lobby of Building A (5660 New Northside Drive NW, Atlanta, GA 30328). Security will have a temporary badge ready for you. Your manager or a team member will meet you in the lobby.

**Q: What should I wear on my first day?**
A: Business casual. A collared shirt with slacks or a skirt is ideal. Dark jeans are acceptable on non-client days after your first week, but for Day 1, lean slightly more formal. No sneakers, athletic wear, or overly casual clothing.

**Q: Where do I park?**
A: Free employee parking is in the attached garage, Levels P2 and P3. On your first day, use visitor parking on Level P1 until you receive your badge (which opens the garage gate). After Day 1, park on P2 or P3.

**Q: What equipment will I get?**
A: Standard setup is a MacBook Pro 16" (or Dell XPS 15 if you prefer), two monitors, a keyboard, mouse, and headset. You'll pick everything up from IT Support (Room 2-104) on your first morning.

**Q: Can I use my own personal laptop for work?**
A: No. All work must be done on ICE-issued equipment for security compliance. You may use your personal phone for Slack notifications (optional) and Duo Mobile (required for 2FA).

## Pay & Compensation

**Q: When do I get my first paycheck?**
A: ICE pays **bi-weekly on Fridays**. Your first paycheck will be on the first Friday pay date that falls at least 2 weeks after your start date. For example, if you start on Monday March 3, your first paycheck would be Friday March 14. Set up direct deposit in Workday on Day 1 to avoid paper check delays.

**Q: How do I set up direct deposit?**
A: Log into **Workday** → Pay → Payment Elections → Add. You'll need your bank routing number and account number. Changes take effect on the next pay cycle.

**Q: Where can I see my pay stubs?**
A: In **Workday** → Pay → Payslips. Pay stubs are available on each payday.

**Q: When do I get a raise?**
A: Merit increases are determined during the **end-of-year performance review** (December). Increases take effect in January of the following year. There is no automatic raise — it's based on your performance rating. For new hires, your first review is at the 6-month mark.

## Benefits & Insurance

**Q: When does my health insurance start?**
A: Coverage begins on the **first day of the month following your start date**. If you start on March 15, coverage begins April 1.

**Q: What if I miss the 30-day enrollment window?**
A: You'll have to wait until **Open Enrollment in November** for the following year, unless you have a qualifying life event (marriage, birth, loss of other coverage). Don't delay — enroll in Workday as soon as possible.

**Q: PPO or HDHP — which should I pick?**
A: If you go to the doctor frequently or take medications, the **PPO** gives predictable copays. If you're generally healthy and want lower premiums plus the HSA tax benefits (and ICE's $1,500 contribution), the **HDHP** is usually the better financial choice. See the Benefits Guide for the full comparison table.

**Q: When does the 401(k) match start?**
A: ICE matches 6% of your salary dollar-for-dollar, starting after your **90-day probationary period**. Your own contributions can start immediately. Enrollment is through **Fidelity NetBenefits**.

## Time Off

**Q: How many PTO days do I get?**
A: 15 days in your first 2 years, 20 days in years 3–5, and 25 days at year 6+. PTO is accrued bi-weekly.

**Q: Can I use PTO during my first week?**
A: Technically yes (PTO accrual starts from Day 1), but it's strongly discouraged during your first month. If you have a pre-planned absence, let your manager know before your start date.

**Q: How far in advance do I need to request PTO?**
A: Submit PTO requests in Workday at least **2 weeks in advance** for planned absences. For same-day sick leave, notify your manager via Slack or email before your scheduled start time.

**Q: Do unused PTO days roll over?**
A: Only **5 unused days** roll over to the next year. The rest expires on December 31. Use it or lose it.

**Q: Are there mental health days?**
A: Yes. ICE provides **2 mental health days per year** in addition to PTO and sick leave. No documentation required — just notify your manager.

## Working at the Office

**Q: Can I work from home?**
A: The QA role requires **full-time on-site presence** (5 days in the office, Monday–Friday). Remote work is not available for this position. Exceptions for medical accommodations require written approval from your VP and HR.

**Q: What are the working hours?**
A: Core hours are **8:00 AM – 5:00 PM EST**. You can flex your start time between 7:00 AM and 9:30 AM as long as you cover core meeting hours (10 AM – 3 PM) and hit 40 hours per week. Your manager must approve your schedule.

**Q: Is there free coffee?**
A: Yes! The coffee bar on the ground floor has **complimentary** coffee, tea, and water available all day. The ICE Café serves subsidized lunches ($5–$10) from 7:30 AM – 2:00 PM.

**Q: Where does the QA team sit?**
A: 3rd Floor, Section C (near the windows). Your exact desk assignment will be arranged by your manager before your start date.

**Q: Where can I take a phone call or focus without distraction?**
A: Quiet rooms and focus pods are available on every floor — no booking required, 2-hour maximum occupancy.

## IT & Tools

**Q: How long does it take to get access to tools (Jira, GitHub, etc.)?**
A: Most ServiceNow requests are processed within **24–48 hours**. VPN and GitHub usually take 24 hours. CyberArk access may take up to **3 business days** due to security review.

**Q: What do I do if my laptop has issues?**
A: Walk in to the IT Help Desk (Room 2-104, 2nd Floor) during business hours (8 AM – 6 PM), call ext. 2-HELP (2-4357), or submit a ServiceNow ticket for non-urgent issues.

**Q: Can I install software on my work laptop?**
A: Personal apps that don't need admin privileges are fine (Spotify, personal browser profile). Development tools or anything requiring admin install needs an IT approval request on ServiceNow.

**Q: How do I connect to the VPN?**
A: Use **Cisco AnyConnect** with your ICE credentials. VPN profiles: `ice-dev-vpn.ice-internal.com` for DEV/QA access, `ice-staging-vpn.ice-internal.com` for STAGING. Multi-factor authentication via **Duo Mobile** is required.
