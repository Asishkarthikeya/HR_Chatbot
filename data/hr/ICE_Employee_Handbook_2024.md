# ICE Employee Handbook 2024

## Core Working Hours

All employees at the Atlanta headquarters are expected to be available during core working hours: **8:00 AM – 5:00 PM Eastern Standard Time (EST)**. Flexible scheduling is permitted outside of core hours, provided your total weekly hours meet the 40-hour requirement and your manager approves your schedule.

- Lunch break: 1 hour, typically taken between 11:30 AM – 1:30 PM
- Flexible start: You may start as early as 7:00 AM or as late as 9:30 AM, as long as core meeting hours (10 AM – 3 PM) are covered
- Time tracking: Log your hours weekly in **Workday** by end of day Friday

## Overtime Policy

- ICE classifies most QA roles as **non-exempt** under the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA)
- Non-exempt employees are eligible for **1.5x overtime pay** for hours exceeding 40 per week
- Overtime must be **pre-approved** by your direct manager before working extra hours
- Exempt employees do not receive overtime but are expected to manage workload within reasonable hours
- Check your offer letter for your FLSA classification — if unsure, contact HR

## Work Location Policy

ICE requires **full-time on-site presence** for this role:

- **5 days in-office** (Monday through Friday) at the Atlanta headquarters
- Remote work is **not available** for this position
- All employees must be physically present at the office during core working hours
- Employees must badge in at the building entrance each day
- Any exceptions (e.g., medical accommodation) require written approval from your VP and HR Business Partner
- Inclement weather: ICE follows the Fulton County government closure schedule. If the county declares an emergency closure, you may work from home with manager notification

## Dress Code

- **Business casual** is the standard dress code for all office days
- No jeans, sneakers, or athletic wear on **client-facing days** (check your team calendar)
- On non-client days, dark jeans with a collared shirt are acceptable
- **Not permitted any day:** shorts, flip-flops, tank tops, ripped jeans, athletic pants/track pants, gym wear
- All employees must wear their ICE badge visibly while on-premises
- If you're unsure whether something is appropriate, err on the side of more formal

## Probationary Period

- All new hires are on a **90-day probationary period** from their start date
- During this period, performance is assessed more frequently (30-day and 60-day check-ins with your manager)
- Employment may be terminated during probation with minimal notice if performance or conduct expectations are not met
- Benefits (401k match, tuition reimbursement) may have separate eligibility start dates — see specific policy sections
- After completing probation, you transition to regular employment status

## Paid Time Off (PTO)

| Tenure | Annual PTO Days |
|--------|----------------|
| Year 1–2 | 15 days |
| Year 3–5 | 20 days |
| Year 6+ | 25 days |

- PTO requests must be submitted in Workday at least **2 weeks in advance** for planned absences
- PTO does not roll over beyond **5 unused days** per year — use it or lose it
- Managers may deny PTO during critical project phases or release windows
- PTO is prorated for employees who start mid-year
- Half-day PTO is available (minimum 4-hour increments)
- Consecutive PTO exceeding 5 business days requires VP-level approval
- PTO is accrued bi-weekly; check your Workday dashboard for current balance

## Sick Leave

- **10 sick days per year**, available from your start date
- Sick leave does **not roll over** to the next calendar year
- A doctor's note is required for absences exceeding **3 consecutive days**
- Sick leave cannot be used as supplemental PTO
- If you're sick, notify your manager via Slack or email **before** your scheduled start time
- Sick leave can be used for your own illness, medical appointments, or caring for an immediate family member

## Parental Leave

- **Primary caregiver:** 16 weeks fully paid parental leave (birth, adoption, or foster placement)
- **Secondary caregiver:** 4 weeks fully paid parental leave
- Eligibility: Must have been employed for at least **12 months** at the time of leave
- Parental leave can be taken consecutively or split into blocks within 12 months of the qualifying event
- Notify HR at least **8 weeks before** anticipated leave start date
- During parental leave, all benefits continue as normal
- Note: Parental leave runs concurrently with FMLA leave where applicable

## Family and Medical Leave (FMLA)

- Eligible employees may take up to **12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave** per 12-month period
- Eligibility: 12+ months of employment and 1,250+ hours worked in the preceding 12 months
- Qualifying reasons: birth/adoption, serious health condition (self or family member), military family needs
- FMLA leave is **unpaid** but you may use accrued PTO or sick leave concurrently
- Contact Jessica Martinez (HR Business Partner) to initiate FMLA — do not submit through Workday self-service
- ICE maintains your health benefits during approved FMLA leave

## Bereavement Leave

- **Immediate family** (spouse, child, parent, sibling): **5 paid days**
- **Extended family** (grandparent, in-law, aunt/uncle): **3 paid days**
- Additional unpaid leave may be approved by your manager on a case-by-case basis
- No documentation is required, but please notify your manager as soon as possible

## Jury Duty & Voting Leave

- **Jury duty:** ICE provides full pay for the duration of jury service — no PTO deduction. Provide your jury summons to HR.
- **Voting:** Up to **2 hours of paid time** on election days (federal, state, or local). Coordinate timing with your manager.

## Health Insurance

- Enrollment must be completed within **30 days of your start date** via Workday
- **Plan options:**
  - Aetna PPO (individual or family coverage)
  - Aetna High-Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) with HSA option
- ICE contributes up to **$1,500/year** to employee HSA accounts on the HDHP plan
- Dental and vision coverage are included in all plans
- Coverage is effective on the **first day of the month following your start date**
- Open enrollment occurs annually in **November** for the following year
- Qualifying life events (marriage, birth, etc.) allow mid-year enrollment changes — notify HR within 30 days

## 401(k) Retirement Plan

- ICE offers a **401(k) plan through Fidelity**
- Company match: **6% of salary**, dollar-for-dollar
- Matching begins after **90 days of employment**
- Employees are immediately vested in their own contributions; company match vests over **3 years** (33% per year)
- Enrollment and fund selection are managed through **Fidelity NetBenefits**
- You can change contribution percentages at any time through Fidelity
- Roth 401(k) option is also available

## Performance Reviews

- Performance reviews are conducted **twice per year**: mid-year (June) and end-of-year (December)
- Rating scale: Exceeds Expectations / Meets Expectations / Needs Improvement / Does Not Meet Expectations
- Reviews are completed through **Workday Performance Module**
- You will complete a **self-assessment** before your manager's review
- New hires receive their first review at the **6-month mark** (instead of waiting for the regular cycle)
- Merit increases and bonus eligibility are tied to end-of-year performance ratings

## Wellness Programs

- **Gym subsidy:** ICE reimburses up to **$75/month** for gym memberships (submit receipts through Concur)
- **Mental health days:** 2 additional paid mental health days per year (separate from PTO and sick leave)
- **Wellness budget:** $500/year for wellness-related expenses (ergonomic equipment, standing desk accessories, blue-light glasses, etc.)
- **On-site wellness:** Quarterly wellness events, flu shot clinic (October), ergonomic assessments
- **Headspace subscription:** Free Headspace premium account for all employees (activate through Workday benefits)

## Tuition Reimbursement

- ICE reimburses up to **$5,250/year** for approved educational expenses
- Eligibility: Must have completed **6 months of employment** and be in good standing
- Qualifying courses must be relevant to your current role or career development at ICE
- Process: Pre-approval required — submit a tuition reimbursement request through ServiceNow before enrollment
- Reimbursement is paid after successful course completion (grade C or above, or pass for pass/fail courses)
- If you leave ICE within 12 months of receiving reimbursement, you may be required to repay the amount

## Employee Referral Program

- Earn **$2,500** for each successful referral who completes 90 days of employment
- Technology and engineering roles may qualify for enhanced referral bonuses of up to **$5,000**
- Submit referrals through the **Workday Recruiting** module
- Referral bonuses are paid on the next payroll cycle after the referred employee completes 90 days
- There is no limit to the number of referrals you can make

## Resignation & Departure

- ICE requests a minimum of **2 weeks written notice** for voluntary resignations
- Submit your resignation letter to your manager and email a copy to HR (jessica.martinez@ice-internal.com)
- During your notice period, you'll work with your manager on knowledge transfer and transition planning
- Exit interview with HR is requested (optional but encouraged)
- Return all ICE equipment (laptop, badge, parking card, headset) to IT on your last day
- Final paycheck (including unused PTO payout) will be issued on the next regular pay cycle
- COBRA health insurance continuation information will be mailed within 14 days of your last day

## Employee Assistance Program (EAP)

- Free, confidential counseling services available **24/7**
- Covers mental health, financial planning, legal consultations, and work-life balance support
- Up to **6 sessions per year** at no cost to you
- Contact: **1-800-555-0199** or via the Workday benefits portal
- Services extend to immediate family members living in your household
