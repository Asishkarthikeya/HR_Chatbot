# ICE Code of Conduct

## Communication Norms

### Slack
- Use Slack for quick questions, informal discussions, and team coordination
- **Never share sensitive data in Slack** — this includes credentials, API keys, customer data, trade information, or MNPI
- Use appropriate channels: #qa-team for team discussions, #qa-automation for technical topics
- Set your status to reflect your availability (e.g., "In a meeting", "OOO", "Focus time")
- Respond to direct messages within **4 business hours** during work hours
- Use threads for extended discussions to keep channels readable

### Email
- Use email for formal communications, cross-department requests, and documentation trails
- All emails containing internal business information must stay within the @ice.com domain
- Do not forward internal emails to personal email accounts
- Use "ICE Confidential" in the subject line for sensitive internal communications
- Email signatures must include your full name, title, and department

### Meetings
- Be on time; if running late, notify via Slack
- Use **Zoom** for all remote/hybrid meetings — do not use unauthorized video platforms (Teams, Webex, etc.)
- Record meetings only with explicit consent from all participants
- Camera on is encouraged but not required for internal team meetings
- External meetings: Camera on is expected

## Material Non-Public Information (MNPI)

**This is critically important at ICE.** As an employee of a financial infrastructure company, you may encounter Material Non-Public Information.

### What is MNPI?
Information that is:
- **Material**: Would a reasonable investor consider it important in making an investment decision?
- **Non-Public**: Has it been broadly disseminated to the general public?

If the answer to both questions is "yes," the information is MNPI and must be handled with extreme care.

### Examples of MNPI at ICE
- Unreleased financial results or earnings data
- Pending mergers, acquisitions, or partnerships
- Trading volumes or clearing data before public release
- New product launches or platform changes before announcement
- Regulatory actions or investigations not yet disclosed
- Significant customer wins or losses not yet announced
- Changes to licensing fees or market data pricing before public notice

### MNPI Rules
- **Do NOT** discuss MNPI outside of secure, authorized channels
- **Do NOT** trade securities based on MNPI (this is insider trading and is **illegal**)
- **Do NOT** share MNPI with friends, family, or anyone outside ICE who doesn't have a business need to know
- **Do NOT** discuss MNPI in public spaces (elevators, cafeteria, airport lounges)
- **Report** any suspected MNPI leaks to the Compliance team immediately
- When in doubt, treat information as MNPI and consult Compliance at compliance@ice-internal.com

### Trading Restrictions
- All ICE employees must pre-clear any trades in ICE stock (or stocks of ICE-listed companies you have MNPI about) through the **Compliance Pre-Clearance Portal** in Workday
- Blackout periods apply around earnings releases — you'll be notified via email when blackout periods begin and end
- Violations of insider trading rules can result in criminal charges, fines, and immediate termination

## Social Media Policy

- **Do not** represent yourself as speaking on behalf of ICE on any social media platform unless authorized by Corporate Communications
- **Do not** discuss ICE business, clients, products, financials, or strategies on social media
- **Do not** share photos of your workspace, screens, or any ICE internal content
- LinkedIn profiles may list ICE as your employer but should not include confidential project details
- If contacted by media or press, direct all inquiries to the **Corporate Communications team** (comms@ice.com)

## Anti-Harassment & Anti-Discrimination

ICE is committed to providing a workplace free from harassment and discrimination.

- ICE prohibits harassment or discrimination based on race, color, religion, gender, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, age, disability, veteran status, or any other legally protected characteristic
- This applies to all work-related settings: office, offsite events, work travel, virtual meetings, and Slack/email
- **What constitutes harassment:** Unwelcome conduct that creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment — including verbal, physical, or visual conduct
- **If you experience or witness harassment:** Report it immediately to your manager, HR Business Partner (Jessica Martinez), or the Ethics Hotline (anonymous)
- ICE has **zero tolerance** for retaliation against anyone who reports harassment in good faith

## Gifts & Entertainment Policy

- Employees may **not accept** gifts valued over **$100** from vendors, clients, or business partners
- Gifts of any value that could create the appearance of a conflict of interest must be declined
- Business meals and entertainment are permitted if they serve a legitimate business purpose and are of reasonable value
- All gifts received must be disclosed annually through the Compliance portal in Workday
- **Never accept:** Cash, gift cards, event tickets from anyone who has pending business with ICE, or luxury items

## Acceptable Use of Company Devices

- ICE-issued laptops and devices are for **business use primarily**
- Limited personal use is permitted (e.g., checking personal email during breaks) but must not interfere with work or violate any policies
- **Prohibited activities:**
  - Installing unauthorized software without IT approval
  - Accessing inappropriate or illegal content
  - Using company devices for personal business ventures
  - Disabling security software (antivirus, endpoint protection, VPN)
  - Connecting to unsecured public Wi-Fi without VPN
  - Storing personal files on the company laptop's hard drive (use personal cloud storage instead)
- ICE reserves the right to monitor and audit company device usage

## Data Classification & Handling

| Classification | Description | Handling | Examples |
|---------------|-------------|----------|----------|
| Public | Press releases, published reports | No restrictions | Annual reports, press releases |
| Internal | Team docs, process guides, wiki pages | Keep within ICE systems | Confluence pages, Slack messages |
| Confidential | Financial data, HR records, trade data | Encrypted storage, need-to-know access | Performance reviews, client contracts |
| Restricted | MNPI, credentials, security configs | Strict access controls, no sharing | API keys, unreleased earnings, production passwords |

- When unsure of a document's classification, **treat it as Confidential** and ask your manager
- Never transmit Confidential or Restricted data via personal email, USB drives, or unauthorized cloud services
- All Confidential and Restricted documents must be stored on ICE-approved systems (Confluence, SharePoint, CyberArk)

## Disciplinary Process

ICE follows a progressive disciplinary process for policy violations:

1. **Verbal warning** — Manager documents the issue and expected correction
2. **Written warning** — Formal written notice placed in employee file via Workday
3. **Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)** — Structured 30/60/90-day improvement plan with measurable goals
4. **Final written warning** — Last chance before termination; may include suspension
5. **Termination** — Employment ends

**Note:** Serious violations (theft, fraud, harassment, MNPI violations, violence) may result in **immediate termination** without progressive steps. For questions about the disciplinary process, contact your HR Business Partner.

## Workplace Violence Policy

- ICE has zero tolerance for workplace violence, threats, intimidation, or bullying
- This includes verbal threats, physical aggression, and threatening messages (email, Slack, text)
- If you feel unsafe: Leave the area immediately and contact Security (ext. 2-1000) or call 911
- All threats are taken seriously and investigated by Security and HR

## Conflicts of Interest

- Disclose any outside employment, board positions, or financial interests that could conflict with your role at ICE
- Annual disclosure is required via the Compliance portal in Workday
- When in doubt, disclose — the Compliance team will advise on whether a conflict exists
- Side projects or freelance work: Permitted as long as they don't compete with ICE, use ICE resources, or interfere with your job performance. Disclose all side projects to your manager.

## Reporting Violations

If you witness or suspect a violation of the Code of Conduct:

1. **Ethics Hotline:** 1-800-555-0188 (anonymous, available 24/7)
2. **Compliance Team:** compliance@ice-internal.com
3. **Your Manager or HR Business Partner**
4. **Online:** ethics.ice-internal.com (anonymous web form)

ICE has a strict **non-retaliation policy** — employees who report concerns in good faith are protected from adverse action. You will not be penalized for raising concerns even if the investigation finds no violation.
